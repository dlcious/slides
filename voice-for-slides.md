# Voice for slides

Slide copy obeys the master Zendesk brand voice (see `zendesk-brand-skill.md` for the full principles). This reference adds slide-specific rules because slides are a constrained medium: every word competes for attention against imagery, and the reader has seconds, not minutes.

## The four slide-copy principles

1. **Cut, then cut again.** Most slide copy is too long on the first draft. If you can say it in fewer words without losing meaning, do. The reader has seconds.
2. **Lead with the verb or the noun that matters.** "We reduced handle time by 30%" beats "There was a 30% reduction in handle time."
3. **One idea per slide.** If a slide's body copy has two ideas connected by "and" or "also," it's two slides.
4. **Write the headline last.** Draft the body, then distill the single takeaway into the headline. Headlines written first tend to be generic.

## Active voice rewrites

| Passive | Active |
|---|---|
| Requests are handled by our team | Our team handles requests |
| A 30% reduction in costs was achieved | We reduced costs by 30% |
| The feature is being rolled out next quarter | We're rolling out the feature next quarter |
| Customer satisfaction is prioritized by Zendesk | Zendesk puts customer satisfaction first |
| Tickets can be resolved with AI | AI resolves tickets |

## Headline patterns that work

| Pattern | Example |
|---|---|
| **Verb-first action** | "Resolve tickets faster" |
| **Number + outcome** | "30% faster resolutions" |
| **Direct address** | "You own the experience" |
| **Juxtaposition** | "Human at scale" |
| **Question** | "What does good service feel like?" |

## Headline patterns to avoid

| Anti-pattern | Example (don't) | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Vague noun phrase | "Customer Experience Excellence" | "Resolve tickets faster" |
| Title Case | "The Future Of Service" | "The future of service" |
| Period on headline | "We're growing fast." | "We're growing fast" |
| Buzzword pileup | "Leveraging synergies to drive paradigm shifts" | "Working together to change how we serve customers" |
| Two ideas joined by "and" | "Faster service and happier customers" | Pick one; make it two slides |

## Body copy patterns

- **Lead with the takeaway, then support it.** First sentence = the point. Second and third = evidence.
- **One sentence per line when possible.** Dense paragraphs on slides don't get read.
- **Use concrete examples.** "A retail customer handling Black Friday" beats "A high-volume customer."
- **Cite specifics, not adjectives.** "Saved 4 hours per agent per week" beats "Significantly saved time."

## Numbers and data

- Write numbers as digits on slides, even small ones: "3 steps" not "three steps." (Exception: starting a sentence — "Three things to remember.")
- Round aggressively. "30%" is more memorable than "29.7%."
- Include the unit with large numbers: "$1.2M" not "1,200,000."
- One hero number per slide.

## Sentence case rules

Sentence case means **only the first word and proper nouns are capitalized.**

- ✅ "The future of customer service"
- ❌ "The Future of Customer Service"
- ✅ "Meet Zendesk AI" *(Zendesk AI is a proper noun)*
- ✅ "Why iPhone users prefer us" *(iPhone is a proper noun)*

## Punctuation

- **No periods on headlines.** Headlines are titles, not sentences.
- **Periods on statement slides** are fine because those are full sentences.
- **No ampersands** (&) unless part of a brand name. Use "and."
- **Em dashes, no spaces:** "fast—not reckless" not "fast — not reckless."
- **Curly quotes on quote slides:** " " not " ".
- **Oxford comma:** use it. "Speed, scale, and simplicity."

## Avoiding exclusionary language

- Avoid gendered language where possible: "the agent" not "he or she"
- Avoid idioms that don't translate: "knock it out of the park," "boil the ocean," "Monday morning quarterback"
- Avoid pop-culture references that date the deck or don't land globally

## Voice examples — on-brand vs off-brand

### Cover slide

❌ Off-brand:
> "Leveraging AI to Drive Customer Service Excellence in 2026"

✅ On-brand:
> "AI that resolves, not redirects"

---

### Statement slide

❌ Off-brand:
> "Our robust platform enables mission-critical stakeholder engagement across the customer lifecycle."

✅ On-brand:
> "We help your customers feel heard, at any scale."

---

### Content slide

❌ Off-brand:
> "Our AI solution has been leveraged by enterprise customers to operationalize a paradigm shift in how support teams utilize their bandwidth to drive customer satisfaction outcomes."

✅ On-brand:
> "Enterprise teams use Zendesk AI to cut resolution time in half. Agents spend less time on repetitive tickets and more time on the ones that need a human."

---

### Quote slide

❌ Off-brand:
> "Zendesk has been instrumental in our digital transformation journey, enabling us to operationalize best-in-class customer experiences across all touchpoints."

✅ On-brand:
> "Zendesk lets our agents solve problems in minutes. Our customers can feel the difference."

---

## One more reminder

**"The customer is always human."**

Every slide is read by a human with a short attention span and a full day. Write like you're talking to one person — because you are.
