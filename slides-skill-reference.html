01 – Installation

Where it lives.

The Zendesk AI Slides skill is a Claude Code skill file. Place it in your project's skills directory or the global skills directory.

Project-specific (recommended)
shell
mkdir -p .claude/skills
cp zendesk-slides-skill.md .claude/skills/
Global (available across all projects)
shell
mkdir -p ~/.claude/skills
cp zendesk-slides-skill.md ~/.claude/skills/

Once installed, the skill activates automatically when Claude detects slide, deck, or presentation work. No manual invocation required.

04 – Copy rules

Six rules, always enforced.

These rules have no geometric enforcement in the template – the AI must apply them. Apply before showing the user. Do not present a draft that violates them.

01
Sentence case for headlines. "This is the headline" not "This Is The Headline."
02
No period on headlines. Headlines and sublines end without punctuation unless they are a question.
03
One idea per slide. If a slide draft has two distinct claims, flag and ask the user whether to split or keep combined.
04
Body text gets periods. Column body, timeline body, section body, product body – full sentences need terminal punctuation.
05
Brevity is the user's call. If a draft is dense, flag it and ask whether to shorten or split. Do not unilaterally cut content.
06
Jargon swaps. Replace leverage → use, utilize → use, stakeholders → the team / customers (be specific), bandwidth → time / capacity.
05 – Draft format

How to present slides.

Each slide draft follows this structure. The format is consistent and machine-parseable. After showing the draft, ask: "Does this structure work, or should I adjust the archetype / split the content?"

slide-draft.md
**Slide [N]: [Archetype] ([parameters])**

Eyebrow: [NN] · [SECTION LABEL]
Headline: [headline text]
Subline: [subline text, if applicable]
Body: [body text or column content]
Image: [image filename from inventory OR placeholder description]
Speaker: [name, title, company – Cover only]
Quote attribution: [name, title, company – Quote only]

Eyebrow rules: every archetype except Closing carries the eyebrow pill. Format is NN · SECTION LABEL. The number ties to the section, not the slide index. The label is identical across every slide in a section.

10 – Anti-patterns

Six things to never do.

The template won't stop these – the AI must catch them. When you notice yourself about to commit one, stop and revise before showing the user.

01
Empty columns. If using Content (columns: 4) but only 3 items exist, reduce to columns: 3. Don't pad with filler.
02
Headline redundancy. The eyebrow says "03 · Customer success," the headline says "Customer success stories." Pick one – don't repeat the section name verbatim.
03
Mixed Timeline granularity. If milestones are years (2023, 2024, 2025), don't insert "Q4 2024" as one milestone. Keep the time scale consistent.
04
Burying the lede. The most important claim goes in the headline. Use the body to support or explain, not to reveal the conclusion.
05
Placeholder proliferation. A deck with 12 slides and 11 placeholders signals assets aren't ready. After 3–4 placeholders, ask if they want to pause drafting.
06
Agenda mismatch. The Agenda lists 5 sections but the deck has 7 section dividers. The agenda is a contract with the audience – if it's wrong, they'll notice.
11 – Session start

For fully autonomous deck building, share the session-start URL. Claude fetches the skill files, token reference, and archetype renders, then confirms it is ready before asking for content.
