01 – Cover
Archetype 01
Cover
speaker: yes/no
When to use
Deck opener. Every deck starts here. Use speaker: yes for talks or webinars. Use speaker: no for leave-behinds.
Layout
White (Coconut) bg. Large Matcha 400 headline top-left. Image right column (755×926px). Speaker block bottom-left.
What to avoid
Don't use Cover as a chapter break. Don't skip it – even internal decks need a clear opener.
02 – Agenda
Archetype 02
Agenda
rows: 3–8
When to use
Deck roadmap. Use early – slide 2 or 3. Agenda items should map 1:1 to Section dividers later in the deck.
Layout
Sesame 100 background. Large 'Agenda' title bottom-left in Matcha 400. Rows right two-thirds: header + subline + number.
What to avoid
Don't use in short decks (under 10 slides). Don't include slide numbers – they change during iteration.
03 – Section divider
Archetype 03
Section divider
body: yes/no
When to use
Chapter break. The headline should match the agenda row verbatim. Use body: yes when the section name is cryptic.
Layout
Licorice background. Optional body text top-left (white, small). Huge white headline bottom-left at 160pt. No Matcha.
What to avoid
Don't use for every slide – it's a beat. Don't write body text longer than two lines.
04 – Statement
Archetype 04
Statement
no parameters
When to use
Bold declarative slide. One big idea, center-aligned. Use for mission statements. Use sparingly – max two per deck.
Layout
Licorice background. Large white headline center-aligned at 80pt Semi Bold. Zendesk wordmark bottom-left.
What to avoid
Don't use if the idea needs explanation. Don't stack Statements. Don't write more than two lines.
05 – Quote
Archetype 05
Quote
portrait imagehanging punctuation
When to use
Customer or partner testimonial. Use in the proof section. Best when the quote is specific and credible.
Layout
White background. Portrait placeholder left (597×926px). Quote text right at 80pt with hanging punctuation. Attribution below.
What to avoid
Don't use internal quotes. Don't use quotes longer than three lines. Real faces build trust – avoid portrait placeholders.
06 – Content
Archetype 06
Content
columns: 1–5media: image/icon/none
When to use
The workhorse. Headline + optional subline + columnar body. Use columns: 1 for narrative, 3–5 for feature lists.
Layout
White background. Headline 80pt Semi Bold. Subline 40pt Regular. Columns equal-width with 32px gaps. Divider tops each column.
What to avoid
Don't pad with empty columns. Don't bury the lede. Max 5 columns – split if more are needed.
07 – Product plate
Archetype 07
Product plate
bg: Matcha 600
When to use
Product feature with large screenshot. Use for demos and feature deep-dives. Matcha 600 signals 'this is the product.'
Layout
Dark green (Matcha 600) background. Headline top-left white. Body bottom-left white. Screenshot right (944×612px).
What to avoid
Don't use for non-product imagery. Don't use without a real screenshot. Body text max three lines.
08 – Data display
Archetype 08
Data display
type: callouts3×2 grid
When to use
Grid of metric callouts – dashboards, ROI summaries, impact reports. Best when numbers tell the story on their own.
Layout
White background. Headline 80pt top. 3×2 grid with alternating Fern/Shamrock backgrounds. Values in bright Matcha.
What to avoid
Only callouts are supported – no charts. Never invent statistics. Sublines max two lines.
09 – Timeline
Archetype 09
Timeline
milestones: 2–4
When to use
Roadmaps, company history, release schedules. Best with 2–4 milestones – more than 4 gets crowded.
Layout
White background. Headline top-left. Horizontal Matcha connector line with dark circles. Headers ALL CAPS BOLD.
What to avoid
Don't mix time granularity. Don't use for non-chronological steps – that's a Content slide.
10 – Closing
Archetype 10
Closing
no eyebrowwordmark only
When to use
Final slide. Pure brand presence. Use when the deck is self-contained and needs no follow-up action.
Layout
Licorice background. Large 'zendesk' wordmark bottom-left in white. No eyebrow. No footer.
What to avoid
Don't end on a data slide – it's abrupt. Don't use mid-deck. If you need a CTA, add a Content slide before this.
